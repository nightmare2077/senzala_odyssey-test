Armada Pulada

Execution: The Armada Pulada is initiated with a spinning motion, similar to the regular Armada kick. However, what sets it apart is the additional jumping or hopping motion. The Capoeirista leaps into the air, spinning around, and executes the spinning kick with one leg while the other leg remains in the air.

Purpose: This kick is often used to create a threat against an opponent or to evade their attacks. Its acrobatic nature makes it visually striking and unpredictable. It can be used to attack an opponent's upper body, head, or shoulders.

Difficulty: The Armada Pulada requires a high degree of agility, balance, and athleticism. It combines the spinning motion of the Armada with the acrobatic element of jumping or hopping. Capoeiristas need to maintain control throughout the motion.

Combination: Capoeiristas often use this kick as part of a sequence that includes other spins, kicks, and acrobatic moves. The combination of various techniques is a hallmark of Capoeira.

Roda Usage: In the Capoeira roda, the Armada Pulada is employed to engage opponents, demonstrate skill, and maintain the game's fluidity and dynamism. It's a move that adds flair to the game and keeps both participants and spectators engaged.

Training: Mastery of the Armada Pulada requires dedicated training. Capoeiristas work on their spinning technique, jumping ability, and coordination to perform this kick effectively.
