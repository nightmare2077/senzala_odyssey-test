Armada com Martelo

Execution: The Armada com Martelo is initiated with a spinning motion, during which the Capoeirista lifts their kicking leg high and strikes with a hammering motion using the heel or the lower shin. It combines the circular sweep of the Armada with the downward striking motion of the Martelo.

Purpose: This kick is designed to target an opponent's upper body, head, or shoulders, making it a powerful offensive move in Capoeira. It can be used to create an effective threat while maintaining good coverage against counterattacks.

Difficulty: The Armada com Martelo requires a high degree of agility, balance, and flexibility. The ability to execute a fast and controlled spin while maintaining power in the kick is a sign of a skilled Capoeirista.

Combination: Capoeiristas often use this kick in combination with other movements and kicks. It can be part of a sequence that includes spins, sweeps, and acrobatic elements. Capoeira is known for its fluid transitions between various kicks and movements.

Roda Usage: In the Capoeira roda, the Armada com Martelo is employed to engage with opponents, demonstrate skill, and showcase the dynamic nature of the art. It's an exciting and visually striking move that adds flair to the game.

Training: Learning to execute the Armada com Martelo effectively requires training and practice. Capoeiristas work on their spinning technique, balance, and strength to master this kick.
