Au sem Mão

Acrobatic Maneuver: "Au sem Mão" is an acrobatic movement that involves performing a cartwheel without the use of hands for support. It's a visually impressive and physically demanding technique.

Upper Body Control: To execute this move, the Capoeirista relies on upper body strength and balance. The hands are typically kept off the ground throughout the cartwheel, which adds an element of challenge and flair to the movement.

Balance and Coordination: Achieving balance and coordination is crucial for the "Au sem Mão." Capoeiristas must maintain control while moving through the cartwheel, as any loss of balance can result in a less graceful execution.

Expressive Element: The "Au sem Mão" is not only a display of physical prowess but also serves as an expressive element in Capoeira. It adds an artistic and dynamic dimension to the game, contributing to the overall aesthetics of the roda (the circle where Capoeira is played).

Transitionary Move: Capoeiristas may use "Au sem Mão" as a transitional move during a Capoeira game. It allows them to change position or direction while maintaining momentum and fluidity.

Rhythm and Music: Like other Capoeira movements, "Au sem Mão" is often performed in sync with the rhythm of the berimbau and other musical instruments, as well as the songs sung in the roda. The music enhances the overall experience and provides a sense of timing and rhythm.

Acrobatic Training: Learning "Au sem Mão" requires rigorous acrobatic training and practice. Capoeiristas gradually develop the strength, flexibility, and body control needed to perform the movement safely.

Safety Precautions: Due to the acrobatic nature of "Au sem Mão," safety precautions are essential. Practitioners should have a good understanding of the technique and train under the guidance of experienced instructors to minimize the risk of injury.

Artistic Variation: Capoeiristas often add their own artistic variations to the "Au sem Mão," incorporating spins, kicks, or other movements to make it unique and creative.
