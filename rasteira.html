Rasteira

Execution: The practitioner usually performs the rasteira by timing the sweep with precision, aiming to catch the opponent off-guard during their movement or attack.

Low Stance: A low and agile stance is crucial for the effective execution of the rasteira. This allows the capoeirista to move swiftly and apply the sweep at the right moment.

Timing and Coordination: Timing is essential in capoeira, and the rasteira requires coordination between the upper and lower body. The sweep should complement the flow of the jogo (game) and be seamlessly integrated into the capoeirista's movements.

Defensive and Offensive Use: The rasteira serves both defensive and offensive purposes. Defensively, it can be employed to evade an opponent's attack by sweeping their legs. Offensively, it can be used to set up further movements or to initiate a takedown.

Combination with Other Techniques: Capoeira is known for its fluid and acrobatic movements. The rasteira can be combined with other kicks, sweeps, and acrobatics to create a dynamic and unpredictable game.

Controlled Contact: Capoeira emphasizes controlled contact, and the rasteira is executed with the intention of taking the opponent down without causing harm. Practitioners aim to maintain a level of control over the sweep.

Adaptability: Capoeira is an adaptable martial art, and the rasteira can be modified and applied in various ways depending on the capoeirista's style, strategy, and the specific context of the jogo.
