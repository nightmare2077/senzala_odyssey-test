Arruda (2003)

(Peixinho)

Mestre Arruda, a true capoeira luminary, has etched his legacy within the vibrant tapestry of Grupo Senzala de Capoeira. With an aura that ignites the roda, his biography dances with moments of dedication and rhythm. Born in the heart of Brazil, Mestre Arruda found his rhythm within capoeira's embrace at an early age. His journey from being a curious student to a seasoned master unfolded like the melodies of a berimbau.

What sets Mestre Arruda apart is his magnetic presence. With every ginga, he tells a story; with every esquiva, he unveils a chapter. His teachings are imbued not just with the techniques of capoeira, but with the wisdom of a life dedicated to the art. A true guardian of tradition, he ensures the spirit of Senzala lives on through each generation he guides.

As for interesting tidbits, rumor has it that Mestre Arruda's first instrument wasn't a berimbau but a pandeiro! Imagine the rhythms that echoed through the streets as he tapped out his earliest capoeira tunes. Additionally, he's known for seamlessly blending traditional capoeira movements with innovative twists, surprising his students and opponents alike.
