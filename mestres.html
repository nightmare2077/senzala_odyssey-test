Mestres de Senzala

Paulo Flores (1963)

Mestre Paulo Flores is a renowned capoeira master and one of the founders of the Senzala de Capoeira group. He has dedicated his life to promoting and preserving the art of capoeira, and is respected for his technical skill and knowledge of the history and traditions of the art.

Learn more...

Rafael Flores (1963)

Mestre Rafael Flores in the Senzala de Capoeira group. He is a highly respected capoeira master and instructor who has dedicated his life to the art of capoeira and its cultural heritage.

Learn more...

Cláudio Danadinho (1970)

Mestre Claudio Danadinho is a highly respected capoeira master and musician who trained under renowned masters such as Mestre João Pequeno and Mestre Pastinha. He is a founding member of the Grupo Senzala de Capoeira and has influenced countless students with his teachings and music.

Learn more...

Gil Velho

Mestre Gil Velho was born in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil in 1944 and began practicing capoeira at the age of 12. He became a student of Mestre Bimba and later joined the group Senzala de Capoeira, becoming its leader and helping to spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally. Mestre Gil Velho was also known for his unique style of capoeira and worked as a social worker and community activist in Salvador.

Learn more...

Gato

Mestre Gil Velho and Mestre Gato are both well-known figures in the capoeira scene. Mestre Gil Velho was a student of Mestre Bimba and helped spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally, while Mestre Gato has almost 50 years of playing capoeira and is one of the leading teachers of Group Senzala, with a branch in Great Britain.

Learn more...

Sorriso

Mestre Sorriso is a skilled capoeira practitioner from Salvador, Brazil who is known for his expertise in both the Angola and Regional styles of capoeira. He is also involved in community outreach programs that use capoeira to empower young people and promote social change.

Learn more...

Garrincha

Mestre Garrincha is a skilled capoeira practitioner and musician from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He became a mestre in 1978 and has taught capoeira all over the world. He is also known for his unique style of playing the berimbau and his proficiency in other martial arts.

Learn more...

Itamar

Mestre Itamar was a highly respected capoeira master who founded the Groupo Senzala de capoeira in the 1960s. He was known for his expertise in the regional style of capoeira and incorporated elements of other styles such as Angola, while also emphasizing the importance of physical training, music, and philosophy in capoeira as a way of life.

Learn more...

Peixinho

Mestre Peixinho is a highly respected capoeira master and one of the founders of the Groupo Senzala. He is known for his expertise in capoeira and his emphasis on physical training, music, and philosophy in capoeira as a way of life. He has also played a significant role in the professionalization of capoeira and has taught and lived capoeira since 1974.

Arruda (2003)

(Peixinho)

Mestre Arruda's legacy shines brightly within Grupo Senzala de Capoeira, his Brazilian roots infusing his journey with rhythm from an early age. His capoeira mastery weaves tales through each movement, and his teachings carry not just techniques but a life's wisdom. He's a guardian of tradition, passing down Senzala's spirit through generations, while his innovative twists and early connection with the pandeiro add layers of intrigue to his captivating story.

Azeite (2005)

(Peixinho)

Mestre Azeite, a living embodiment of Grupo Senzala's essence, electrifies the roda with movements that seem to possess a life of their own. His teachings transcend technique, delving into capoeira's history and philosophy, ensuring Senzala's legacy thrives within every capoeirista. As legend goes, his capoeira journey took root beneath a majestic baobab tree, where the berimbau's melodies intertwined with his earliest movements, sparking his profound bond with the art.

(Ramos)

Grilo (2009)

(Garrincha)

Mestre Grilo, originally from Rio de Janeiro, discovered Capoeira at the age of 13 through his sister Fernanda. Trained by Mestre Garrincha and Mestre Sorriso, co-founders of Escola Senzala, he established Grilocapoeira in Bremen in 1991 after studying physical education. Awarded the title of Capoeira master in 2009, he now resides in Amsterdam and continues to teach in Bremen. Contramestre Quase has led Grilocapoeira Bremen since 1999, with Contramestre Los Andes, Graduada Seda, and Vassourinha also instructing Capoeira for youth.

Learn more...

Bruzzi (2009)

(Garrincha)

Mestre Bruzzi, originally from Rio de Janeiro, started his Capoeira journey at 14 under Mestre Arara's guidance. He later trained with Mestre Camisa and found his calling with Mestre Garrincha's school. Over the years, he taught beginners, earned a degree in physical education, and taught Capoeira to children. In the early 1990s, he witnessed Capoeira's global expansion and later returned to Europe to further its growth, particularly in the south of France, collaborating with Mestre Sorriso. His work reflects Capoeira's evolution from a Brazilian art form to a worldwide phenomenon.

Pelé (2011)

(Peixinho)

Mestre Adriano da Silva, known as Pelé, hails from Niteroi, Rio de Janeiro. Starting his capoeira journey at age 8, he trained under the tutelage of a founding master of Senzala group in Rio de Janeiro. His dedication culminated in receiving the prestigious red cord, the highest rank within Senzala, in 2011. Mestre Pelé's expertise has led him to teach capoeira across Brazil, Portugal, and Italy for over two decades, enriching communities and sharing his passion in a myriad of settings.

Steen (2011)

(Peixinho)

Steen Møller's lifelong commitment to excellence in physical pursuits shaped his journey as a capoeirista. Introduced to the world of carnival and capoeira at 12 by Ana Pompeia, his path blended samba, taekwondo, and dance. Immersed in capoeira's essence, encounters with Mestre Toni Vargas and Mestre Peixinho ignited his passion. Founding Senzala school in 1991, he balanced capoeira with studies in sports and later embarked on a transformative path as a physical therapist, earning expertise in dance therapy and communication techniques. His graduation as Mestre de Capoeira in 2011 and over three decades as a performer, teacher, and festival organizer globally have cemented his esteemed position in capoeira.

(Gato)

Mestre Pedro's capoeira journey began at 7, deeply rooted in his father's, Mestre Gato, influence. He's cultivated a vibrant capoeira community in the UK, supervising schools across various cities and even branching to international territories like Indonesia and Iran. Capoeira, an integral part of his life, has opened doors to diverse experiences, from exploring Rio's hidden corners to international travels and community work, reflecting his enduring dedication to the art.

Chão (2012)

(Arruda)

Mestre Chão, initially introduced to capoeira on the beaches of Itaipù, Niteroi, at 17, embraced the art and learned under Professor Arruda's guidance. Over the years, he attained various levels of expertise, from blue to red rope, and actively contributed to capoeira's growth in Europe and Brazil through teaching, organizing festivals, and mentoring numerous students and groups. Nega (2017)

(Flávio)

Alessandra, also known as Nega, joined the world of capoeira in 1986 when she was just 7 years old. Her nickname, Nega, was given to her by a friend, Loba, due to being the only black girl in the Senzala Group at the time. She comes from a family of capoeiristas, with her father, mother, and brothers also practicing capoeira. Over the years, Alessandra has traveled the world thanks to capoeira and is thrilled to see more women involved in the art. She emphasizes that capoeira transcends race and color, being a powerful spiritual current.
